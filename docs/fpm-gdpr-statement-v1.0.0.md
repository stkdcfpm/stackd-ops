## Lawful Basis for Processing Personal Data

FPM International processes personal data relating to suppliers and buyers in the course of its trade intermediary activities. This processing is necessary for the performance of contracts to which the data subjects are party, or in order to take steps at their request prior to entering into a contract (Article 6(1)(b) UK GDPR). Specifically, FPM International collects and processes the following categories of data for the following purposes:

**Supplier data** (name, company name, email address, telephone number, country of operation): processed for the purpose of sourcing, procuring and ordering goods from suppliers on behalf of buyers, and for the issuance of purchase orders. Retention period: 6 years from the date of the last transaction, in accordance with HMRC record-keeping requirements for Self Assessment and VAT purposes.

**Buyer data** (company name, contact name, billing address, delivery address, email address): processed for the purpose of issuing commercial invoices, managing payment, arranging delivery of goods, and maintaining financial records. Retention period: 6 years from the date of the last transaction.

Where processing is not strictly necessary for contractual performance, FPM International relies on legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR) — specifically the legitimate interest of maintaining accurate business records, pursuing outstanding payments, and complying with legal obligations under UK tax law.

FPM International does not sell, share, or transfer personal data to third parties except where necessary for the performance of the contract (e.g. freight forwarders, insurers, customs agents) or where required by law.

Data subjects have the right to access, rectify, restrict processing of, or request erasure of their personal data by contacting FPM International at the address above. Data subjects also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.
